Motivation:
I founded my non-profit Tech2gether after seeing several teams around me in the GTA that almost had to 
shut down—not because students lacked talent or commitment, but because they ran out of funding for 
registration fees, parts, travel, and basic equipment. 

The Budget & Access Planner (by Tech2gether) is practical tool that helps teams both plan a season 
financially and get targeted suggestions for sponsorships and grants that can support them. 

This project aims to address the broader social issue of unequal access to STEM education. Robotics 
is one of the most effective hands-on exposures to STEM for high school students, but when participation 
is limited by a team’s ability to pay thousands of dollars per season, students, especially girls, from 
low-income communities lose the opportunity to build skills, confidence and a real sense of belonging in 
STEM fields. High school is a key stage when students discover what they want to pursue in the future, so 
improving access to programs like robotics can directly increase the likelihood that students continue 
pursuing STEM in post-secondary education and future careers.
